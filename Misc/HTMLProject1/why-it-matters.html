Fires


Wildfires are a major concern and will become an even bigger problem if nothing is done to slow them down. Even though wildfires would still burn even if global warming was not real, they are burning with greater intensity than in recorded human history. Wildfire season lasted for 5 months in the 1970s and went to 7-8 months. The temperature itself is not the only factor, and the drought is a problem.

Because there are no vaccines for these diseases because they are not around anymore, they can continue to spread quickly.

Water

Water is becoming unevenly distributed around the world. In certain parts of the world, there is less water in some places, but there is more water than others. This is because of the heat taking water out of areas that tend to be further from the ocean and instead tending to favor the areas closer to the oceans.
As the regions of the world seperate, they become increasingly divided in terms of water exposure.
